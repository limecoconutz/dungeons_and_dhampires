In the Principality of Veltharyn, your bloodline is your biography. The sire house that produced you determines not only your supernatural gifts but your social position, your allies, your most likely enemies, and the shape of your Hunger. A dhampire of House Umbrath and a dhampire of House Ferrum Nox may stand in the same room and have almost nothing in common but the cold edge of their heartbeats.

The six bloodlines below each correspond to one of the great sire houses of the Crimson Council. A player may also choose to be a _Thornborn_ — a dhampire of unknown or mixed bloodline — for whom the table below represents fragments, instincts, and inherited uncertainty rather than clean inheritance.

## The Six Houses and Their Gifts

House Umbrath

The Shadow-Weave

"We do not hide in darkness. We are it."

The eldest of the sire houses, Umbrath dhampires manifest the most visually dramatic bloodline gift: the ability to partially dissolve into shadow, extend darkness as a semi-solid substance, and communicate through darkness with others of their line. They appear pale to the point of translucency, their veins visible in low light like a roadmap drawn in ink. The Hunger of an Umbrath dhampire is not violent — it is patient. It accumulates. It whispers rather than roars, until one day you realize how long it has been since you felt warm.

Primary GiftShadow step, darkness extension, shadow communication

The Hunger Manifests AsA growing indifference to mortal emotion; a desire to watch rather than act

WeaknessBright light causes mounting penalties; sunstone weapons deal radiant damage

House ReputationFeared. The oldest name. Other houses show deference they don't entirely feel.

House Lacrimae

The Mind-Broken

"Every secret you keep, we already know."

House Lacrimae's dhampires inherit a gift as useful as it is corrosive: the ability to sense emotional residue in spaces and people, project false memories, and — at high Hunger — read surface thoughts without consent. They are the empire's preferred interrogators, diplomats, and spies. Their eyes shift color with emotional state: silver when calm, amber when hungry, something that has no name when the Hunger is fully awake. Lacrimae dhampires frequently develop secondary personalities — fragments of the minds they've touched, which speak up at inopportune moments.

Primary GiftEmotion sense, false memory projection, surface thought reading (high Hunger only)

The Hunger Manifests AsIntrusive access to others' memories; difficulty distinguishing self from sensed minds

WeaknessLoud emotional environments cause psychic bleed; crowds are overwhelming

House ReputationDistrusted by all other houses, indispensable to all of them. They know it.

House Ferrum Nox

The Iron-Blooded

"Pain is a language. We are fluent."

The warrior house. Ferrum Nox dhampires carry a blood that calcifies under stress — their skin temporarily hardens, their reflexes accelerate past human limits, and damage that would stagger anyone else reads to them as information about how to better position themselves next time. They are built for war and trained accordingly. Many have the shortest life expectancy of any bloodline — not because they die easily, but because they are sent first into every dangerous engagement. The Hunger of a Ferrum dhampire is physical and urgent, a fire in the muscles that demands feeding after every exertion.

Primary GiftSkin hardening, reflexive acceleration, pain-to-information conversion

The Hunger Manifests AsPhysical craving that intensifies with combat; fighting becomes feeding instinct

WeaknessSilver disrupts the calcification; silver weapons ignore hardened skin bonuses

House ReputationRespected. Used. Often dead before forty. The house produces new ones quickly.

House Caelith

The Tide-Touched

"All things return to us, eventually."

The strangest of the bloodlines — Caelith dhampires inherit a connection to entropy itself. They age backwards when they feed (a process they can control, with effort). They can sense the age of objects and people by touch, accelerate or decelerate decay in organic matter, and at high levels, temporarily reverse damage by reversing time in a small area. House Caelith is based on the western coast and has always had a relationship with the sea that the other houses find unsettling. Their dhampires often have an air of having seen something before, even when they demonstrably have not.

Primary GiftAge sense, entropy manipulation, localized time reversal

The Hunger Manifests AsCompulsive pattern-completion; disturbing knowledge of how things will end

WeaknessStasis magic and preservation spells cause physical pain; antimagic fields disorient

House ReputationConsidered philosophical to the point of uselessness by other houses. They endure.

House Morden

The Ashwalkers

"We remember what burning feels like. It makes us careful."

House Morden was nearly destroyed in the Compact Wars — their original keep was burned to the foundation by a mortal uprising that came closer to succeeding than the official histories acknowledge. The house rebuilt with a new focus: immunity to fire, control over flame, and an uncanny ability to sense betrayal before it fully forms. Morden dhampires carry old ash in their bloodline — a trace of the burning their ancestors survived. They are the most paranoid of the bloodlines and, counterintuitively, the most loyal once they decide you are worth trusting. The Hunger comes with a warmth that mimics genuine emotion and is nothing of the sort.

Primary GiftFire immunity, flame control, betrayal-sense (emotion precognition)

The Hunger Manifests AsFalse warmth; the Hunger feels like affection until it becomes something else entirely

WeaknessCold damage bypasses resistances; frozen environments slow bloodline abilities by half

House ReputationViewed as the weakest house. They are working on changing this. Quietly, carefully.

House Vexillum

The Alchemical

"The body is a formula. We have been solving it for centuries."

The most academically-inclined of the sire houses, Vexillum dhampires can alter their body chemistry in real-time — producing venom, antitoxins, accelerated healing compounds, or paralytic agents from their own blood. They are the empire's alchemists and physicians, and the only bloodline with an explicit tolerance policy toward mortal scholars (useful, the house finds, when you want someone else to test your formulas). The Hunger of a Vexillum dhampire is intellectual as much as physical: a need to understand the thing they consume, which makes them thorough and dangerous feeders.

Primary GiftBlood chemistry alteration: venom, antitoxin, paralytic, accelerated healing

The Hunger Manifests AsAnalytical fixation on prey; the urge to understand before consuming

WeaknessPurifying magic disrupts body chemistry; holy water deals additional damage

House ReputationRespected for utility. Their wards are the most likely to be borrowed by other houses.

DM Note: Mixed Tables and Bloodline Tension

If your party contains dhampires from multiple houses, take advantage of the inherent political tension — they are technically rivals, possibly former allies from their respective training cohorts, and each carries the expectations of a house that may have competing interests. This creates built-in drama without requiring the DM to manufacture it. The best bloodline conflicts emerge when players' in-character loyalties diverge from what they personally want to do.

## The Thornborn: No House, All Heritage

A Thornborn dhampire carries an unknown or deliberately obscured bloodline — the child of a sire who has hidden, died, or refused to claim their offspring. Within Veltharyn's structure, this is a precarious position: without a house patron, a Thornborn has no legal standing, no protection, and no formal training. What they have is flexibility, anonymity, and the possibility of manifesting traits from multiple bloodlines as the campaign progresses — a wildcard nature that the houses find deeply threatening.

A Thornborn player chooses one starting bloodline gift, but gains additional gifts from other bloodlines through story events rather than level-ups — reflecting a nature that is still discovering itself. This is a higher-complexity option recommended for players who have played at least one other bloodline first.
